Top of Funnel

Contrasting Viewpoints

User Segment: Tech-First News Consumers

Who: Early adopters with high tech literacy
Characteristics: Value innovation and multiple perspectives
Journey Stage: Immediate power user potential
"If we assume our top-funnel, tech-first news consumer power users strongly prefer the 'Opposite Sides' summary style (based on 90% of unique user selections thus far), it suggests they value multiple perspectives and technical depth. This insight could inform our social strategy by creating content that mirrors this style—emphasizing contrasting viewpoints while showcasing Particle's unique AI capabilities. By leading with technical transparency and balanced analysis, we can further validate these users' interest in both innovation and comprehensive, unbiased news consumption."

Strengths

  • Aligns content with user preferences
  • Showcases Particle's unique AI capabilities
  • Emphasizes balanced analysis

Considerations

  • Ensure objectivity in contrasting viewpoints
  • Balance depth with digestibility
  • Continuously validate through engagement metrics

Feature Mix & Customization

"If we assume our top-funnel, AI enthusiast power users prefer to engage with a mix of product features, it suggests they value customization and personalization in how they consume content. This insight could drive our social strategy by encouraging experimentation with diverse content formats and frameworks that offer distinct entry points and perspectives. By creating a variety of content styles—ranging from in-depth analyses to quick summaries—we can align with these users' preferences and allow them to self-select the value they derive from Particle."

Strengths

  • Caters to users' desire for control over content consumption
  • Allows users to self-select their value
  • Provides multiple entry points for engagement

Considerations

  • Maintain cohesive brand voice across formats
  • Clearly communicate purpose of each content style
  • Monitor engagement to identify most effective formats

Middle Funnel: Educational Simplicity

User Segment: Core News Readers & Tech-Curious

Who: Traditional news readers and tech newbies
Characteristics: Value structured information, require education
Journey Stage: Exploring features, building trust
"If we assume our middle-funnel, core users—comprising traditional news readers and tech newbies or skeptics—require more education and trust-building as they explore Particle's features, our social strategy for this segment could prioritize clear and digestible educational content. By breaking down complex topics into approachable formats, we can help bridge the gap between traditional news consumption and AI-powered insights. In doing so, we could explore leveraging less popular but simpler summary styles like 'Bullet Points' (selected by 52% of unique users) and 'Five Ws' (selected by 40% of unique users). These summary styles suggest a preference for structured, clear information delivery, which may resonate with users who value straightforward explanations. This approach could serve to build trust and ease skepticism—both about news consumption in the current climate and the evolving role of AI technology."

Educational Simplicity Strengths

  • Prioritizes clear and digestible educational content
  • Addresses unique needs of less AI-familiar users
  • Builds trust through transparent explanations

Key Considerations

  • Ensure educational content remains engaging
  • Use relatable examples and case studies
  • Incorporate user feedback to refine content

Bottom Funnel: Cautious Connections & Rejectors

User Segment: Cautious Consumers

Who: Most cautious and skeptical audience
Characteristics: Low trust in AI, value traditional methods
Journey Stage: Potential non-users, need education
"If we assume bottom-funnel users are our most cautious and skeptical audience—potentially with low interest in traditional news consumption—we can anticipate that many will remain non-users. However, non-user education and reputation building remain critical for driving consideration over time. Some of these individuals may eventually move up the funnel, while others may still come to understand and advocate for the product's value, even if they never use it themselves. For example, men understand the value of tampons without being users of the product. With this in mind, our social strategy need not prioritize this segment immediately. Instead, our focus should remain on educating and engaging early adopters and core users. As we scale and acquire larger off-platform media budgets, this bottom-funnel segment can be targeted through broader awareness campaigns, such as OOH placements and ATL initiatives, to address their concerns and build trust on a larger scale."

Strengths

  • Prioritization: Acknowledges challenges in converting highly skeptical users
  • Reputation-Building: Recognizes importance of non-user education
  • Future-Proofing: Sets stage for potential future consideration

Potential Risks

  • Missed Advocacy Opportunities: Some cautious users may convert earlier with focused trust-building
  • Brand Perception Gap: Ensure these users don't perceive Particle as overly niche or exclusionary

Future Considerations

  • Non-User Content Examples:
    • Case studies showing successful real-world usage
    • Transparent breakdowns of AI accuracy and bias mitigation
  • Longer-Term Planning:
    • Include non-user education in future campaigns
    • Frame as complementary rather than primary effort
