Who is Particle for? | Particle Audience Mapping

1. FEATURE USAGE HYPOTHESES
Our early data suggests certain feature usage patterns may indicate specific user preferences and behaviors:
  • Opposite Sides selection suggests users who value balanced perspectives and are willing to engage with contrasting viewpoints
  • Multiple feature engagement in a single session likely indicates power user potential
  • Q&A usage may indicate either top of funnel and middle of funnel engagement patterns or both, with bottom of funnel likelihood being low
  • Story completion through full cluster scrolling could indicate deeper engagement and comprehension
  • KGE following behavior might signal specific topic interest and likelihood of return visits
  • Audio version usage could indicate users seeking efficiency or alternative consumption methods
  • External link exploration may suggest users wanting deeper understanding of topics
  • Summary style preferences (Bullet Points, Five Ws) might indicate how users prefer to process information
2. ASSUMED USER BEHAVIORS BASED ON FEATURES
We're basing our early assumptions on a combination of early Mixpanel analytics, intuitive & anecdotal assumptions, employee usage patterns, feedback from our Slack community, and social listening. Here's what we believe about our user segments:
  • Power Users via AI Interest & High Tech Adoption:
    • Feature Engagement: These users are actively experimenting with every new AI feature we release, often pushing the boundaries of whatâ€™s possible with our platform.
    • Feedback: They provide detailed feedback on technical capabilities, helping us refine features and optimize performance.
    • Advocacy: As platform innovators, they naturally advocate for new features and share their experiences with others, helping to expand our user base.
    • Feature Exploration: They are instrumental in identifying new feature opportunities, constantly exploring advanced features like AI-powered summaries, personalized topic recommendations, and multi-feature integrations.
  • Power & Core Users via News Interest & Medium to High Tech Adoption:
    • Feature Usage: These users engage with multiple formats (e.g., summaries, audio versions, visual content) to deeply understand stories, leveraging the diverse ways our platform presents information.
    • Engagement: They regularly engage with contrasting viewpoints and summary styles (like Opposite Sides), showing a preference for balanced, well-rounded perspectives.
    • Efficiency & Depth: They value the combination of efficiency (quick summaries, audio) and depth (in-depth analyses, comprehensive features like Q&A).
    • Consistency: They build consistent usage habits by sticking to their preferred features, such as summary style formats and following specific knowledge graph entities (KGEs).
  • Core & Casual Users via Feature Exploration with Low to Medium Tech Adoption:
    • Onboarding & Progression: These users are primarily focused on understanding the value of each core feature. Features like the basic summary style (bullet points or Five Ws) help them grasp the platformâ€™s value.
    • Quick Wins: They tend to experience immediate benefits from the basic features, such as reading short summaries or listening to audio versions, before advancing to more complex features.
    • Feature Growth: Their journey involves clear progression paths, like moving from simple summary styles to exploring more sophisticated features (e.g., Opposite Sides summaries or multi-KGE tracking) as they become more comfortable with the platform.

  • Social media engagement patterns and comments
  • Employee usage patterns (as early adopters themselves)

    • Social engagement patterns reveal content format preferences
4. STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS & UNLOCKS
By understanding our user segments' behaviors and needs, we can align our product and marketing strategies more effectively. Here are some strategic insights:
  • Targeted User Engagement: We can develop personalized engagement strategies based on how each user segment interacts with the platform, targeting Power Users for early access and advocacy, while nurturing Core and Casual Users with educational content and feature progression.
  • Feature Optimization: Identifying which features drive the most engagement helps prioritize future product updates, focusing on features like Q&A or Opposite Sides summaries for deeper user engagement.
  • Content Strategy: By understanding the dual needs for efficiency and depth among Power and Core Users, we can develop content that balances both quick summaries and in-depth analyses to enhance user satisfaction.
  • Continuous Feedback Loop: The interconnected feedback system ensures that the platform evolves in real-time based on user data, allowing us to refine our product and marketing strategies to meet user needs effectively.
