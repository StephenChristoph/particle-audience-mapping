1. FEATURE USAGE HYPOTHESES
Our early data suggests certain feature usage patterns may indicate specific user preferences and behaviors:
  • Opposite Sides selection suggests users who value balanced perspectives and are willing to engage with contrasting viewpoints
  • Multiple feature engagement in a single session likely indicates power user potential
  • Q&A usage may indicate either top of funnel and middle of funnel engagement patterns or both, with bottom of funnel likelihood being low
  • Story completion through full cluster scrolling could indicate deeper engagement and comprehension
  • KGE following behavior might signal specific topic interest and likelihood of return visits
  • Audio version usage could indicate users seeking efficiency or alternative consumption methods
  • External link exploration may suggest users wanting deeper understanding of topics
  • Summary style preferences (Bullet Points, Five Ws) might indicate how users prefer to process information
2. ASSUMED USER BEHAVIORS BASED ON FEATURES
We're basing our early assumptions on a combination of early Mixpanel analytics, employee usage patterns, feedback from our Slack community, and social listening. Here's what we believe about our user segments:
Power Users via AI Interest
These users represent an interesting opportunity: While they might not typically be heavy news consumers, their enthusiasm for AI technology drives high engagement. Think of them like tech enthusiasts who discover a new way to consume news - the AI element is the hook that pulls them in and keeps them engaged. They're likely to:
  • Experiment with every new AI feature we release
  • Provide detailed feedback on technical capabilities
  • Become advocates for the platform's innovation
  • Help us identify new feature opportunities
Power & Core Users via News Interest
These users come to us primarily seeking better news consumption, and the AI features enhance their experience rather than drive it. They're similar to people who switched from traditional cable to streaming services - they want the same content, but through a better delivery system. We see them:
  • Using multiple formats to deeply understand stories
  • Regularly engaging with contrasting viewpoints
  • Valuing both efficiency and depth
  • Building consistent usage habits
Core & Casual Users via Feature Exploration
These users are our "try before you buy" audience. They're curious but cautious, needing clear evidence of value before deepening their engagement. Think of them like people testing a new productivity app - they'll start with basic features and only expand their usage if they see clear benefits. We need to:
  • Provide clear pathways to understand each feature's value
  • Demonstrate immediate benefits from basic features
  • Create obvious progression paths to more advanced features
3. TESTING APPROACH
We're gathering insights through an interconnected feedback system:
  • Real usage data from early app adoption via Mixpanel analytics
  • Direct feedback from our engaged Slack community
  • Social media engagement patterns and comments
  • Employee usage patterns (as early adopters themselves)
  • Alignment with our core company value proposition

    • Marketing touchpoint responses help refine audience understanding

1. FEATURE ENGAGEMENT DATA
Our most significant early data points come from summary style selection patterns, which directly inform our content marketing approach:
  • Opposite Sides summary style (90% of unique user selections) suggests users value balanced, comprehensive content
  • Bullet Points format (52% of unique user selections) indicates preference for clear, structured information
  • Five Ws format (40% of unique user selections) shows appreciation for traditional news framework
These selections can occur multiple times within any given cluster. This tells us not just how users consume news, but specifically how they prefer to engage with content. These preferences are crucial because:
  • They inform how we should structure our marketing messages
  • They guide our voice and tone across all marketing touchpoints
  • They help us match content formats to user preferences
  • They reveal how different segments prefer to process information
Think of it like this: If a user prefers bullet points in the app, they'll likely respond better to clear, structured marketing messages. If they use Opposite Sides frequently, they might engage more with marketing that presents multiple perspectives.
2. ASSUMED BEHAVIORS BASED ON PRODUCT FEATURES
Each feature interaction gives us clues about user preferences and potential:
Power User Indicators
We're looking at several key behaviors that suggest power user potential:
  • Story Completion: We're building tracking to measure full cluster completion (think of this like tracking if someone reads an entire article vs just the headline)
  • Feature Combinations: Users who mix multiple features in one session, especially combining Opposite Sides with other features, showing they're extracting maximum value
  • Exploration Patterns: Their willingness to try external links and audio versions suggests they're looking to deeply understand topics
  • Q&A Engagement: We're studying how Q&A usage might indicate product-market fit (similar to how commenting on articles shows deeper engagement with traditional news)
Core User Patterns
These users show different but equally important engagement patterns:
  • Feature Focus: They tend to find and stick with specific features that work for them, similar to how people might always read the sports section first in a traditional newspaper
  • Gradual Exploration: Unlike power users who dive into everything, they methodically try new features over time
  • Consistent Usage: They develop regular patterns with their preferred features, suggesting they've found clear value
  • Q&A Potential: Their Q&A usage might indicate different things than power users, helping us understand how this feature serves different segments

Top of Funnel

Contrasting Viewpoints

User Segment: Tech-First Consumers, Regular News Consumers, & AI Enthusiasts

Who: Early adopters with high tech literacy
Characteristics: Value innovation and multiple perspectives
Journey Stage: Immediate power user potential
"If we assume our top-funnel, tech-first news consumer power users strongly prefer the 'Opposite Sides' summary style (based on 90% of unique user selections thus far), it suggests they value multiple perspectives and technical depth. This insight could inform our social strategy by creating content that mirrors this style—emphasizing contrasting viewpoints while showcasing Particle's unique AI capabilities. By leading with technical transparency and balanced analysis, we can further validate these users' interest in both innovation and comprehensive, unbiased news consumption."

Strengths

  • Aligns content with user preferences
  • Showcases Particle's unique AI capabilities
  • Emphasizes balanced analysis

Considerations

  • Ensure objectivity in contrasting viewpoints
  • Balance depth with digestibility
  • Continuously validate through engagement metrics

Feature Mix & Customization

"If we assume our top-funnel, AI enthusiast power users prefer to engage with a mix of product features, it suggests they value customization and personalization in how they consume content. This insight could drive our social strategy by encouraging experimentation with diverse content formats and frameworks that offer distinct entry points and perspectives. By creating a variety of content styles—ranging from in-depth analyses to quick summaries—we can align with these users' preferences and allow them to self-select the value they derive from Particle."

Strengths

  • Caters to users' desire for control over content consumption
  • Allows users to self-select their value
  • Provides multiple entry points for engagement

Considerations

  • Maintain cohesive brand voice across formats
  • Clearly communicate purpose of each content style
  • Monitor engagement to identify most effective formats

Middle Funnel: Educational Simplicity

User Segment: Core News Readers & Tech-Curious

Who: Traditional news readers and tech newbies
Characteristics: Value structured information, require education
Journey Stage: Exploring features, building trust
"If we assume our middle-funnel, core users—comprising traditional news readers and tech newbies or skeptics—require more education and trust-building as they explore Particle's features, our social strategy for this segment could prioritize clear and digestible educational content. By breaking down complex topics into approachable formats, we can help bridge the gap between traditional news consumption and AI-powered insights. In doing so, we could explore leveraging less popular but simpler summary styles like 'Bullet Points' (selected by 52% of unique users) and 'Five Ws' (selected by 40% of unique users). These summary styles suggest a preference for structured, clear information delivery, which may resonate with users who value straightforward explanations. This approach could serve to build trust and ease skepticism—both about news consumption in the current climate and the evolving role of AI technology."

Educational Simplicity Strengths

  • Prioritizes clear and digestible educational content
  • Addresses unique needs of less AI-familiar users
  • Builds trust through transparent explanations

Key Considerations

  • Ensure educational content remains engaging
  • Use relatable examples and case studies
  • Incorporate user feedback to refine content

Bottom Funnel: Cautious Connections & Rejectors

User Segment: Cautious Consumers

Who: Most cautious and skeptical audience
Characteristics: Low trust in AI, value traditional methods
Journey Stage: Potential non-users, need education
"If we assume bottom-funnel users are our most cautious and skeptical audience—potentially with low interest in traditional news consumption—we can anticipate that many will remain non-users. However, non-user education and reputation building remain critical for driving consideration over time. Some of these individuals may eventually move up the funnel, while others may still come to understand and advocate for the product's value, even if they never use it themselves. For example, men understand the value of tampons without being users of the product. With this in mind, our social strategy need not prioritize this segment immediately. Instead, our focus should remain on educating and engaging early adopters and core users. As we scale and acquire larger off-platform media budgets, this bottom-funnel segment can be targeted through broader awareness campaigns, such as OOH placements and ATL initiatives, to address their concerns and build trust on a larger scale."

Strengths

  • Prioritization: Acknowledges challenges in converting highly skeptical users
  • Reputation-Building: Recognizes importance of non-user education
  • Future-Proofing: Sets stage for potential future consideration

Potential Risks

  • Missed Advocacy Opportunities: Some cautious users may convert earlier with focused trust-building
  • Brand Perception Gap: Ensure these users don't perceive Particle as overly niche or exclusionary

Future Considerations

  • Non-User Content Examples:
    • Case studies showing successful real-world usage
    • Transparent breakdowns of AI accuracy and bias mitigation
  • Longer-Term Planning:
    • Include non-user education in future campaigns
    • Frame as complementary rather than primary effort
